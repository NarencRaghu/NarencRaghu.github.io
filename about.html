Computational Biology and Machine Learning

I have always been fascinated by the world of biology, specifically genetics. My other passion for coding drove me to learn Machine Learning. By combining my two passions, I found the realm of Computational Biology, where Computer Programming meets Biology. I want to create a world where the rates of prevalent diseases can be decreased because of the interdisciplinary collaboration between people and machines.

My ultimate dream is to improve analysis and treatment of diseases through revolutionary improvements in biomedical technologies and computational analysis.

Computational Biology is a rising power because of the recent improvements in data collection in the fields of genomics and biomedical imaging. The resulting surplus of biological data requires the use of advanced computational tools to build integrated models of biological processes that can be used to prevent or treat disease.

My Background.

I am currently a rising senior in Scottsdale, Arizona. I have taken 17 AP courses and in doing so encountered AP Biology and AP Computer Science Principles, where I realized my passions. Within the challenging AP Biology class, I encountered the field of genetics where many diseases originate. Because of my immense interest in genetics, as well as computer science, I have decided to pursue a combination of both in the field of computational biology, with an ultimate goal of becoming a doctor.
